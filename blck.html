What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so extreme that nothing—not even light—can escape. They represent the most intense warping of spacetime known in the universe.

Structure of a Black Hole

Event Horizon: The boundary beyond which escape is impossible.

Singularity: A point of infinite density.

Accretion Disk: Superheated matter spiraling inward.

Photon Sphere: Region where light orbits the black hole.

Formation

Black holes form when massive stars collapse after exhausting nuclear fuel. The core implodes, creating a singularity while outer layers explode as a supernova.

Physics & Relativity

Black holes are governed by Einstein’s general relativity. They bend spacetime, slow time, and create extreme gravitational effects.

Time dilation: Time moves slower near the event horizon.

Spaghettification: Objects are stretched by gravity.

Hawking Radiation: Black holes slowly evaporate.
